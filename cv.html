Research Experience

  • Ocean-Atmosphere Coupling in the North-East Tropical Atlantic and its Impact on Precipitations in Senegal (PhD thesis from Gaston Berger University of Saint-Louis (Senegal) and Sorbonne University (France)). In this thesis, I demonstrated that ocean-atmosphere coupling in the North-East Tropical Atlantic (NETA) significantly influences precipitation patterns in Senegal.
  • Phenology and Early Formation Mechanisms of Upwelling Along the Southern Coast of Senegal (Master's thesis internship in 2021 at the LSAO-MED Laboratory, Saint-Louis, Senegal). The objective of this study was to investigate the interannual variability of upwelling in southern Senegal (referred to as the Petite Côte) using the NEMO ocean model and observations from 1990 to 2015. The findings were published in the journal AJEST in December 2021.
  • Evaluation of Large-Scale Vertical Velocities in the Tropical Atlantic: Analysis of Linear Vorticity Balance (Master's thesis internship in 2020 at the LOCEAN Laboratory, Paris, France). The primary objective of this study was to reconstruct oceanic vertical velocities using the linear vorticity balance (LVB) equation, based on outputs from the NEMO general ocean circulation model over a 25-year period (1980-2004). The findings were published on the AVISO+ website.
