Research Experience

  • Ocean-Atmosphere Coupling in the Northeast Tropical Atlantic and its Impact on Precipitations in Senegal (October 2020 - March 2024 doctoral thesis, Sorbonne University, France, and Gaston Berger University of Saint-Louis, Senegal).
    In this thesis, I demonstrated that ocean-atmosphere coupling in the Northeast Tropical Atlantic (NETA) region significantly influences precipitation in Senegal. An analysis of 40 years of monthly data and 20 years of daily data revealed a regional mechanism occurring between July and September: a weakening (or strengthening) of the northeast trade winds induces a warm (or cold) sea surface temperature (SST) anomaly in the NETA. This SST anomaly, in turn, influences precipitation in Senegal via a geostrophic adjustment of the low-level West African jet, which either enhances (or reduces) the transport of moisture to the continent.
  • Phenology and Early Formation Mechanisms of Upwelling Along the Southern Coast of Senegal (Master's thesis internship in 2021 at the LSAO-MED Laboratory, Saint-Louis, Senegal).
    The objective of this study was to investigate the interannual variability of upwelling in southern Senegal (referred to as the Petite Côte) using the NEMO ocean model and observations from 1990 to 2015. The findings were published in the journal AJEST in December 2021.
  • Evaluation of Large-Scale Vertical Velocities in the Tropical Atlantic: Analysis of Linear Vorticity Balance (Master's thesis internship in 2020 at the LOCEAN Laboratory, Paris, France).
    The primary objective of this study was to reconstruct oceanic vertical velocities using the linear vorticity balance (LVB) equation, based on outputs from the NEMO general ocean circulation model over a 25-year period (1980-2004). The findings were published on the AVISO+ website.
